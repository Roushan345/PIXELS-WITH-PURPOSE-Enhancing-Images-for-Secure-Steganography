🔒 Steganography — How It Works (LSB Method)

01

Cover Image

Select any digital image (PNG/JPG) as the carrier. More pixels = more hiding capacity.

02

Text → Binary

Each character is converted to its 8-bit ASCII binary. E.g. 'A' = 01000001

03

LSB Replacement

The least significant bit of each pixel's R/G/B channel is replaced with one bit of the secret.

04

Stego Image

The result looks identical to the eye — each pixel changes by at most 1 (out of 255).

🔍 Steganalysis — Chi-Square Attack

01

Pair of Values

Group pixel values into pairs (0,1), (2,3), (4,5)… LSB embedding makes even & odd pairs equal.

02

Chi-Square Statistic

χ² = Σ (Observed − Expected)² / Expected. High embedding = low χ², suspicious distribution.

03

p-value

p-value < 0.05 → strong statistical evidence of hidden data (less than 5% chance it's natural).

04

LSB Plane Visual

Visualizing the LSB plane reveals noise-like randomness in stego images vs. natural images.
